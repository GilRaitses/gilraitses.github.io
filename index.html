About Me

I work at the intersection of computational science, machine learning, and geospatial simulation. My focus is on developing physics-informed and foundation models that extract meaningful patterns from complex signals across biological, behavioral, and environmental domains. Through innovative algorithmic pipelines, I transform raw sensory and spatial data into scalable computational frameworks that enable deeper analysis, clearer interpretation, and more accurate predictive simulations of natural phenomena.

Current Work

  • Marine Telemetry Modeling: Large-scale data pipelines for detecting behavioral states in whale movement using dive-derived signal features.
  • Neural Circuit Signal Analysis: Exploring sensory integration and emergent perception using pattern recognition on neural data.
  • Glacial Geophysics: Reconstructing ice flow and terrain evolution using remote sensing, simulation modeling, and multi-resolution GIS analytics.
