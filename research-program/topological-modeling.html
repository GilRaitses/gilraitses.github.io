Topological modeling

This preview translates the Manhattan graph work into mathematical language and keeps its scope honest as a modeling frame with results still in progress.

Why topology helps

This work represents the city as a graph whose directed edges carry measured quantities drawn from real conditions. A route is a trace through that graph, and a loop exposes structure that a single route passes over.

Paths, loops, and residuals

The distinction that does the modeling work here is the one between a path and a loop. A route comparison shows a path choice, while a closed walk asks whether local costs cancel or leave a residual.

Public-safe boundary

Topology serves here as modeling language, and the note keeps all of its claims at that level. The computed homology groups, a completed fundamental-group analysis, and a final theorem about Manhattan remain future work.
