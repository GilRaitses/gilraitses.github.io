Spatial media installation

This preview shows how the Manhattan graph-field work can become a spatial media environment that a viewer moves through and reads directly.

Installation frame

The installation concept uses maps, monitors, color, light, route traces, and room-scale movement to make a measured city field perceptible. The viewer encounters the graph as an environment with regions, thresholds, and changing route conditions.

The heatmap provides a public-facing bridge from camera-derived measurement to a spatial field. It gives the installation a concrete source object grounded in real measurement.

Why it belongs with the research

The installation page extends the same research question into perception and media: how can a measured field be made visible enough for a person to move through, compare, and judge?
