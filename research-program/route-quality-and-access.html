Route quality and neighborhood access

This preview explains the Manhattan mobility work in practical terms: two addresses can look close on a map while the lived route between them carries different walking burden, exposure, and daily convenience.

What the page shows

The work compares route quality, street access, corridor load, and neighborhood fit using measured street conditions as the basis for every comparison.

The first panel shows the same origin and destination under two different routing assumptions, where a similar map length can still produce a very different accumulated exposure along the way.

The second panel shows local street constraints that accumulate across a path. It connects the work to neighborhood choice, route quality, and future location-intelligence tools.
